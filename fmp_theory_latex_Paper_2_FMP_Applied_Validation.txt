While the Fractal Metascience Paradigm (FMP) provides a robust theoretical framework for recursive knowledge systems, its practical viability depends on empirical validation through real-world implementation. The six-hour co-creation session between human researcher and AI assistant produced 37 technical modules processing over 120,000 tokens with 75.7\% structural validation—empirically confirming the recursive co-construction hypothesis.

This paper establishes that FMP is not theoretical abstraction but operational reality—a verified methodology for building adaptive, ethically embedded, and self-organizing knowledge systems at scale.

\end{abstract}
